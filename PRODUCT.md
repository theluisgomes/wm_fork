# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Marketing directors, C-suite executives, and agency planners at mid-to-large Brazilian companies. They evaluate consultancies for strategic data intelligence, brand positioning, and product development partnerships. They visit this site during research and vendor evaluation, often on large monitors in office environments, sometimes on mobile during commutes. They value substance, craft, and proof of capability over flashy trends.

## Product Purpose
Wisemetrics' corporate landing page. It exists to communicate the company's positioning at the intersection of data science, strategic consulting, and digital product development. Success means a visitor understands the three service fronts, sees the methodology as rigorous, trusts the case studies, and contacts the team.

## Brand Personality
Precise, incisive, grounded. The voice of a strategist who has seen the data and knows where to move. Not academic, not startup-hype, not agency-cool. Confident without arrogance, technical without jargon.

## Anti-references
- Generic SaaS landing pages with hero-metric dashboards and purple gradients
- Cookie-cutter agency sites with parallax hero images and "we're creative" copy
- Consulting sites that feel like PowerPoint decks (McKinsey blue, stock photography grids)
- Glassmorphism-heavy dark themes with neon accents (crypto/Web3 aesthetic)

## Design Principles
1. **Show the thinking, not the tools.** Every visual element should communicate intellectual depth, not technical decoration.
2. **Earned confidence.** Let the work (case studies, methodology, team) do the talking. Design supports credibility; it doesn't manufacture it.
3. **Signal over noise.** The brand literally converts noise into signal. The design should embody that: clarity emerging from complexity, not complexity for its own sake.
4. **Distinct but not alien.** This is a Brazilian consultancy working with established brands. The visual language should feel sophisticated and modern without alienating corporate decision-makers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (already implemented)
- Ensure readable contrast ratios on dark backgrounds
- Support keyboard navigation throughout
